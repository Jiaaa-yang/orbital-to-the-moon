Our AI

Artificial intelligence forms the core of our web application. In particular, we have trained two machine learning models to use in our application. We make use of scikit-learn for this purpose.

The first model is a Random Forest Classifier which predicts whether the stock of a price will rise. This model is in charge of the main results shown whenever you search for the analysis of a stock. In this case, the confidence level is a measure of the probability which the model thinks its prediction is correct, based on data points it has previously seen.

The second model is a LinearSVC model which classifies tweets into those that have a positive financial sentiment, and those with negative financial sentiments. While there are many pre-trained sentiment analysis models out there, not many of them are fine-tuned for financial texts. Try comparing the sentiment classifier meant for normal text below (using vaderSentiment's classifier), and the sentiment classifier that we have trained. You can see that our classifier is more suited for financial text, or tweets in our case.

Polarity score is a value between -1 and 1, where 1 represents the most positive sentiment

The confidence score represenst the distance from the decision hyperplane in the LinearSVC model, where a higher value means the model is more confident of its prediction
